Thanks for visiting! I'm Austin Parker, a software dev from Los Angeles. I made this website to showcase my projects and catalog lessons learned.

## Background
 I started coding back in my second year of college. I still had no idea what I wanted to study back then, leaning toward history just because it was interesting. My mom "gently" suggested that I find a job with more financial stability. I agreed because I was lazy...also because she's scary, man.
 
My first CompSci professor was amazing. He eased us into the subject while stoking our interest in learning more. I was *really* bad at first, struggling with simple concepts like recursion. My first "this is awesome" moment was slogging through a tough homework until I finally got my solution working. It was frustrating at first, but the goalposts were clear and each attempt brought me closer. Finally getting there felt awesome! It scratched that puzzle-solving itch and convinced me I could make it in Computer Science. I was hooked after that - I switched majors and started my STEM adventure. Catching up on credits was hell on earth, but that's a story for another time. 

So here I am! I landed my first internship in web development and continued down that path. I've worked on teams with different tech stacks, business goals, and group dynamics. My work is typically split about 70/30 between the backend and frontend, though I'm comfortable working full-time in either. I'm also studying game design, machine learning, and Japanese to open some doors. This site should help me brainstorm ways to apply those skills. 

## Values
1. Curiosity. Learn something new every day, even if it's just memorizing a flashcard. Always keep a sense of forward momentum.
2. Flexibility. Avoid getting bogged down in routines that make the world seem smaller than it actually is. Try to meet new people and find new opportunities.
3. Resilience. Don't pretend failure doesn't matter. Reflect on it and bounce back.
